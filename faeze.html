what are ABS?

ABS, short for abdominal muscles, are a group of muscles located in the front of your stomach. These muscles not only give your midsection a sculpted appearance when toned, but they also play a critical role in stabilizing your body, maintaining posture, and supporting your movements. A strong set of ABS enhances your athletic performance and protects your lower back from injury.


Types of Abdominal Muscles

  • Rectus Abdominis: Known as the "six-pack," this muscle runs vertically along the front of your abdomen and is responsible for flexing your spine and creating a flat, toned appearance.

  • External Obliques:These muscles run diagonally on each side of your abdomen, helping with side bending and twisting movements.

  • Internal Obliques: Located beneath the external obliques, these work in conjunction to support your spine and assist in rotational movements.

  • Transverse Abdominis:The deepest layer of abdominal muscles, it acts like a corset around your midsection, providing core stability and protecting your internal organs.
